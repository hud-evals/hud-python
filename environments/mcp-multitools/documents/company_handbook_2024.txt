CHAPTER 2: EMPLOYMENT POLICIES
============================================

EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY
Stellar Dynamics is committed to equal employment opportunity. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic.

HIRING PROCESS
All positions require:
1. Initial screening by HR
2. Technical assessment (where applicable)
3. Panel interview with hiring manager
4. Final interview with department head
5. Background check and reference verification
6. Offer letter and onboarding

EMPLOYMENT CLASSIFICATION
- Full-time: 40+ hours per week, benefits eligible
- Part-time: 20-39 hours per week, prorated benefits
- Contractor: Project-based, no benefits
- Intern: Temporary educational assignment

PROBATIONARY PERIOD
All new employees undergo a 90-day probationary period during which:
- Performance is closely monitored
- Regular feedback sessions occur (weekly)
- Employment may be terminated without notice
- Benefits enrollment begins on day 31

AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT
Employment with Stellar Dynamics is at-will. Either party may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice, subject to applicable laws.

CHAPTER 3: WORK HOURS & REMOTE WORK
============================================

STANDARD WORK HOURS
Core business hours: 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM (local time)
Standard workday: 8 hours
Standard workweek: 40 hours (Monday-Friday)

FLEXIBLE SCHEDULING
Employees may adjust their start and end times provided they:
- Are available during core hours (10 AM - 4 PM)
- Complete their required hours
- Coordinate with their team
- Obtain manager approval

REMOTE WORK POLICY
Effective January 2024, Stellar Dynamics operates under a hybrid work model.

REMOTE WORK ELIGIBILITY
Employees may work remotely if they:
- Have completed their probationary period
- Have manager approval
- Have a suitable home office environment
- Meet performance expectations
- Have reliable internet (minimum 50 Mbps)

REMOTE WORK SCHEDULE
- Engineering teams: Up to 4 days remote per week
- Sales teams: Up to 3 days remote per week
- Customer support: Up to 2 days remote per week
- Executive staff: As needed with board approval
- Maximum consecutive remote days: 10 without manager approval

IN-OFFICE REQUIREMENTS
All employees must be in-office:
- For quarterly all-hands meetings
- During annual planning week (first week of December)
- For client-facing meetings when requested
- Minimum 1 day per week for collaboration

HOME OFFICE STIPEND
Remote-eligible employees receive:
- One-time setup allowance: $1,500
- Monthly internet reimbursement: $75
- Annual equipment refresh: $500

INTERNATIONAL REMOTE WORK
Working from outside your home country requires:
- Written approval from HR
- Maximum duration: 30 days per calendar year
- Compliance with local tax and labor laws
- VPN usage at all times
- Notification to IT Security

CHAPTER 4: COMPENSATION & BENEFITS
============================================

PAY SCHEDULE
- Salaried employees: Semi-monthly (15th and last day)
- Hourly employees: Bi-weekly (every other Friday)
- Commission: Monthly, following month-end close

SALARY BANDS
Level 1 (Entry): $55,000 - $75,000
Level 2 (Intermediate): $70,000 - $95,000
Level 3 (Senior): $90,000 - $130,000
Level 4 (Lead/Manager): $120,000 - $175,000
Level 5 (Director): $160,000 - $225,000
Level 6 (VP): $200,000 - $325,000
Level 7 (C-Suite): $300,000+

EQUITY COMPENSATION
Stock options vest over 4 years with a 1-year cliff:
- Year 1: 25% vests at 12-month anniversary
- Years 2-4: Monthly vesting (2.083% per month)

HEALTH INSURANCE
Coverage begins on the first day of the month following hire date.

Plans offered:
- PPO Premium: $0 employee / $450 family monthly
- PPO Standard: $0 employee / $300 family monthly
- HMO: $0 employee / $200 family monthly
- HSA-eligible HDHP: Company contributes $1,500 annually

DENTAL & VISION
- Dental: 100% preventive, 80% basic, 50% major
- Vision: Annual exam covered, $200 frame allowance

RETIREMENT
401(k) plan with company match:
- 100% match on first 3% of salary
- 50% match on next 2% of salary
- Maximum company match: 4% of salary
- Immediate vesting

ADDITIONAL BENEFITS
- Life insurance: 2x annual salary (up to $500,000)
- Short-term disability: 60% of salary, 26 weeks max
- Long-term disability: 60% of salary
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Commuter benefits (pre-tax)
- Gym membership: $50/month reimbursement
- Professional development: $2,500 annual budget
- Tuition reimbursement: Up to $10,000/year

CHAPTER 5: LEAVE POLICIES
============================================

PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
PTO accrual based on tenure:
- Years 0-2: 15 days per year (1.25 days/month)
- Years 3-5: 20 days per year (1.67 days/month)
- Years 6-10: 25 days per year (2.08 days/month)
- Years 11+: 30 days per year (2.5 days/month)

Maximum PTO carryover: 5 days per year
PTO request notice: 2 weeks for 3+ consecutive days

SICK LEAVE
- 10 paid sick days per year
- No carryover; resets January 1
- May be used for self or family member care
- Doctor's note required after 3 consecutive days

COMPANY HOLIDAYS (2024)
1. New Year's Day - January 1
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day - January 15
3. Presidents' Day - February 19
4. Memorial Day - May 27
5. Independence Day - July 4
6. Labor Day - September 2
7. Thanksgiving - November 28
8. Day after Thanksgiving - November 29
9. Christmas Eve - December 24
10. Christmas Day - December 25
11. New Year's Eve - December 31

PARENTAL LEAVE
Birth parent: 16 weeks paid leave
Non-birth parent: 8 weeks paid leave
Adoption/Foster: 12 weeks paid leave

Leave must be taken within 12 months of birth/placement.
May be taken consecutively or intermittently with manager approval.

BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
- Immediate family: 5 paid days
- Extended family: 3 paid days
- Close friend: 1 paid day

JURY DUTY
Full pay for up to 10 days of jury service per year.
Must provide court documentation.

VOTING LEAVE
Up to 2 hours paid time off to vote if polls are not open outside of work hours.

MILITARY LEAVE
In accordance with USERRA. Differential pay for up to 12 months.

CHAPTER 6: CODE OF CONDUCT
============================================

PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR
All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues with respect and dignity
- Maintain professional communication
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Protect company assets and information
- Report violations promptly

HARASSMENT & DISCRIMINATION
Stellar Dynamics has zero tolerance for:
- Sexual harassment
- Discriminatory behavior
- Bullying or intimidation
- Retaliation against reporters

Report concerns to HR or use the anonymous hotline: 1-888-555-ETHC

SUBSTANCE ABUSE
- Drug-free workplace policy applies
- Alcohol permitted only at company-sanctioned events
- Marijuana use prohibited regardless of local laws
- Prescription medications with manager/HR awareness if affecting work

CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
Employees must disclose:
- Outside employment
- Financial interests in competitors/vendors
- Family relationships with business contacts
- Board positions or consulting arrangements

GIFTS & ENTERTAINMENT
- May accept gifts under $100 in value
- Must report gifts over $100 to Compliance
- Never accept cash or cash equivalents
- Entertainment must be reasonable and business-related

SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
When discussing company matters on social media:
- Clearly identify opinions as your own
- Never share confidential information
- Do not disparage competitors
- Follow SEC guidelines regarding stock discussion

CHAPTER 7: IT & SECURITY POLICIES
============================================

ACCEPTABLE USE
Company technology is provided for business purposes.
Limited personal use is permitted if it:
- Does not interfere with work
- Does not violate any policies
- Does not consume excessive resources
- Does not expose the company to risk

PASSWORDS
Requirements:
- Minimum 12 characters
- Must include: uppercase, lowercase, number, symbol
- Changed every 90 days
- No password reuse (last 12 passwords)
- Multi-factor authentication required for all systems

DATA CLASSIFICATION
- Public: Marketing materials, press releases
- Internal: Policies, procedures, org charts
- Confidential: Financial data, employee records
- Restricted: Customer data, source code, trade secrets

DATA HANDLING
Restricted data must be:
- Encrypted at rest and in transit
- Accessed only on approved devices
- Never stored on personal devices
- Deleted according to retention schedule

SECURITY INCIDENTS
Report immediately to security@stellardynamics.com:
- Suspicious emails or phishing attempts
- Lost or stolen devices
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Malware or virus detection

REMOTE ACCESS
VPN required for:
- Accessing internal systems remotely
- Using public WiFi
- International travel
- Home network connections to work systems

BYOD (BRING YOUR OWN DEVICE)
Personal devices may access company resources if:
- Enrolled in MDM (Mobile Device Management)
- Running approved operating system version
- Using company-approved security software
- Capable of remote wipe if lost/stolen

CHAPTER 8: TRAVEL & EXPENSES
============================================

TRAVEL APPROVAL
- Domestic travel: Manager approval
- International travel: VP approval
- Travel during blackout periods: C-suite approval

BOOKING PROCEDURES
All travel must be booked through Concur Travel.
Book at least 14 days in advance for best rates.

AIRFARE
- Domestic: Economy class
- International (under 6 hours): Economy Plus
- International (6+ hours): Business class
- Upgrades at employee expense unless approved

LODGING
Maximum nightly rates (before tax):
- Tier 1 cities (NYC, SF, London): $350
- Tier 2 cities (Chicago, Seattle, Paris): $275
- Tier 3 cities (all others): $200
- Extended stay (5+ nights): Use corporate housing

GROUND TRANSPORTATION
- Rental cars: Compact class unless business need
- Ride-share: Permitted for business travel
- Personal vehicle: $0.67 per mile (2024 IRS rate)

MEALS & INCIDENTALS
Daily limits (domestic):
- Breakfast: $20
- Lunch: $30
- Dinner: $60
- Incidentals: $25

International rates vary by location - see Appendix C.

EXPENSE REPORTING
- Submit within 30 days of travel completion
- Original receipts required for amounts over $25
- Itemized receipts required for all meals
- Manager approval required before reimbursement
- Reimbursement processed within 10 business days

NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
- Personal entertainment
- Mini-bar charges
- In-room movies
- Gym access fees
- First-class upgrades (unless pre-approved)
- Childcare or pet-sitting
- Traffic tickets or parking violations

CHAPTER 9: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
============================================

PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE
Annual reviews conducted in Q1 (January-March)
Mid-year check-ins in July

RATING SCALE
5 - Exceptional: Consistently exceeds expectations
4 - Exceeds: Regularly exceeds expectations
3 - Meets: Consistently meets expectations
2 - Developing: Sometimes meets expectations
1 - Below: Does not meet expectations

GOAL SETTING
Employees must have:
- 3-5 performance goals
- At least 1 development goal
- SMART criteria (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Alignment with team and company objectives

MERIT INCREASES
Based on performance rating:
- Rating 5: 8-12% increase
- Rating 4: 5-7% increase
- Rating 3: 2-4% increase
- Rating 2: 0-1% increase
- Rating 1: No increase; PIP required

PROMOTIONS
Eligibility requirements:
- Minimum 12 months in current role
- Rating of 4+ in most recent review
- Manager recommendation
- Budget availability
- Skills assessment completion

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)
Triggered by:
- Rating of 1 or 2
- Consistent performance issues
- Behavioral concerns

PIP duration: 30-90 days
Review frequency: Weekly
Outcomes: Improvement, extended PIP, or termination

CHAPTER 10: TERMINATION PROCEDURES
============================================

VOLUNTARY RESIGNATION
- 2 weeks notice required (4 weeks for managers)
- Submit resignation in writing to manager and HR
- Exit interview scheduled by HR
- Final paycheck within applicable state timeframe

INVOLUNTARY TERMINATION
May result from:
- Performance issues
- Policy violations
- Position elimination
- Business restructuring

SEVERANCE
Standard severance (involuntary, non-cause):
- 1 week per year of service
- Minimum: 2 weeks
- Maximum: 26 weeks
- COBRA subsidy: 3 months

No severance for:
- Voluntary resignation
- Termination for cause
- Probationary period terminations

RETURN OF COMPANY PROPERTY
On last day, return:
- Laptop and peripherals
- ID badge and access cards
- Company credit cards
- Keys and parking passes
- Documents and files

FINAL PAY
Includes:
- Salary through last day worked
- Accrued, unused PTO
- Any unpaid bonuses or commissions
- Expense reimbursements

REFERENCES
HR will confirm:
- Dates of employment
- Job title
- Salary (with written authorization)

Performance details not disclosed without legal requirement.
